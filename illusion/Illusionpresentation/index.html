**CHAMFER** the systematic quality was very present in the concept of the typeface so its use fit well. as you can see aside from the use of negative space, this element is very prevelent in the overall design. the chamfer does have historical ties with large-scale lettering: they often used simple letterforms when working large, since they can be scaled up more easily this is where the grid became useful and instrumental, requiring fewer calculations for curves.

FORM

“Form is the three-dimensional counterpart to shape. Shape is to form as a square is to a cube. Form is shape with dimension or volume.”
~Others


Rectangular forms, in different weights are used as base elements of the typeface.

By playing with these elements the illusion of depth is infered and some light & dark forms begin to present themselves

**FORM** I aimed to focus on the form and shape of the design. By that I mean that I was looking to make a graphic shape. However I found that when you set up a grid system, these decisions are at times, automatically decided, sometimes not for the better. For example, on some of the edges, for optical reasons, adjustments had to be made. the T just did not need chamfers on such a small crossbar. all this allowed me to learn more about the process of making letterforms and have a new appreciation! crazy!

DECISIONS

The angles 120º and 60º not used in the same order or placements throughout the typeface as other fonts.

monospace parameters allow the stem to be the same weight when used as a diagonal or horizontal stem.

The numbers used for the stems outline and the inset enhance the vision of the illusion shapes and its dimensions.

**DECISIONS** Some of the decisions which help in defining the typeface characters are; the angles 120º and 60º which are not used in the same order or placements throughout the typeface as other fonts. not all corners have an angle as is the base idea behind most of the chamfered typefaces. I struggled with this rule a bit and sticking to it didn’t make sense in all glyphs. Thanks Yujune! The stem is also the same weight when used as a diagonal , horizontal stem or crossbar. The measurements used for the outline and inset are intended to interact with the visibility of the shadow form & it’s dimension.
